Humans live on Mars
is it possible?

Mars might not be somewhere we could live. We are not sure how effective Mars's atmosphere would be as a radiation shield (how will astronauts hide from radiation on Mars?) And we must remember that the explorers will have to spend a long time on the planet. There can be no quick there-and-back dash, as with the Moon.

  1. 01

    Mars: The Red Planet

    Mars, often referred to as the Red Planet, is the fourth planet from the Sun in our Solar System. It is named after the Roman god of war due to its reddish appearance, which comes from iron oxide (rust) on its surface. Here's a detailed overview:

    • Distance from the Sun: About 227.9 million kilometers (141.6 million miles).
    • Length of a Day (Sol): 24.6 hours.
    • Length of a Year: 687 Earth days.
    • Diameter: 6,779 kilometers (4,212 miles) – roughly half the size of Earth.
    • Gravity: 38% of Earth's gravity (you'd weigh much less on Mars!).
    • Moons: Two small moons, Phobos and Deimos.
  2. 02

    Interesting Facts About Mars

    • Mars has the largest volcano in the Solar System, Olympus Mons, which is about 21.9 km (13.6 miles) high.
    • Valles Marineris, a canyon system on Mars, stretches over 4,000 km (2,500 miles).
    • Mars experiences massive dust storms that can last for weeks and cover the entire planet.
    • Sunsets on Mars appear blue due to the way its atmosphere scatters sunlight.
    • Mars' atmosphere is 95.3% carbon dioxide, making it inhospitable for human life without support systems.
  3. 03

    Water on Mars

    Evidence suggests Mars once had flowing rivers, lakes, and even an ocean. Today, water is mostly found as ice in polar caps and possibly beneath the surface.

    • Seasonal dark streaks on the surface hint at possible briny liquid water flows.
    • Subsurface ice could be a vital resource for future human missions.
  4. 04

    Exploration of Mars

    Several missions have explored Mars, including orbiters, landers, and rovers.

    • Rovers: Perseverance, Curiosity, Spirit, and Opportunity have provided invaluable data.
    • Orbiters: Mars Reconnaissance Orbiter and MAVEN continue to study the planet from above.
    • Future: Plans for human exploration by NASA and private companies like SpaceX are underway.
